## Ondes Martenot
	- The Ondes Martenot is an early electronic musical instrument invented in 1928 by French cellist and radio engineer Maurice Martenot. Known for its ethereal, voice-like timbre, it became a favorite among avant-garde composers and remains one of the most expressive electronic instruments of the 20th century.
	- ### Key facts
		- **Invented:** 1928, Paris, France
		- **Inventor:** Maurice Martenot
		- **Sound generation:** Heterodyne oscillators (radio-frequency mixing)
		- **Play method:** Keyboard and ring-controlled pitch ribbon
		- **Notable composers:** Olivier Messiaen, André Jolivet, Jonny Greenwood
