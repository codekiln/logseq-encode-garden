- # Goodhart's Law
	- **Statement**: once a measure becomes a target, it stops being a good measure—people optimize the metric rather than the thing it was meant to track.
	- **Origin**: economist Charles Goodhart (1975), originally about monetary policy; later generalized to its common form by Marilyn Strathern.
	- **Kind**: heuristic.
	- **Example**: rewarding developers for "lines of code" yields more code, not necessarily better software.
	- ## See also
		- [[Campbell's Law]] — a stronger, sociological version of the same failure.
		- [[McNamara Fallacy]] — the companion error of measuring only what is easy.
