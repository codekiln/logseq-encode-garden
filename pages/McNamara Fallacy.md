- # McNamara Fallacy
	- **Statement**: relying only on what is easily measured while ignoring important factors that are hard to measure—and treating the unmeasured as unimportant.
	- **Origin**: named for Robert McNamara and the use of body counts as a metric of progress during the Vietnam War.
	- **Kind**: named fallacy.
	- **Example**: judging a war's progress by enemy body count while ignoring whether the strategy was working.
