- # Platformer
	- **Overview** — A **platformer** (platform game) is a game genre where, like in [[Co/Nintendo/Game/Super Mario]], traversal and timing matter: the player moves a character through **levels** made of **platforms**, **gaps**, and **verticality**, usually with **running** and **jumping** as core verbs. Fail states often come from **falls**, **hazards**, or **enemies** rather than from abstract puzzle rules alone.
	- **Context** — The label spans **2D** side-scrollers, **3D** action-platformers, **precision** (“hard”) platformers, and hybrids such as **Metroidvania** (platforming plus ability-gated map progression). Not every game with platforms is “a platformer” in the catalog sense; the genre tag fits when **jumping-led exploration or obstacle courses** are the main loop.
	- **Links** — Wikipedia’s [Platform game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platform_game) article is a reasonable orientation anchor for vocabulary and history.
