- # [Accumulation of Corpuscules](https://sites.inagrm.com/avdg/activities/accumulation.xhtml)
	- ## Description
		- In [[Granular Synthesis]] the grains are beyond the threshold of auditory perception
		- This energy model functions through *accumulation*; it's about the *Process* not about a *Form*
		- ### The "Image-Model" of a sound
			- Depending on the scale of perception, the same sound source can be *corpuscular* or a *flow*:
				- The sea (close = corpuscle-like froth, far off = flow), with water in general it's the same idea
				- A crowd on a football field (from far away = corpuscles, those close to you = detailed events, shouts, etc.)
				- Wind in leaves (close = corpuscles, far = flow)
		- We hear something global in the whole collection of grains
		- We can hear the mini-particles that stand out from the whole (for example, those generated by aluminum foil) by focusing on the high frequencies
		- ### Aspects
			- [[Grain]]
			- [[Mass]]
			- [[Pitch]]
			- [[Caliber]] - size from large to very small
			- [[Density]] of accumulations are factors of perceptual variations in the accumulation of corpuscles.
			- Think of the "noise" current in electronic signals as well
	- ## Commentary
		- There's a difference between [[Friction]] and accumulation of corpuscles
		- If [[Friction]] generates individual or iterative grains, or if the grain is so fine that it approaches a smoothness of sound, then *accumulation of corpuscles* refers to when the perception focuses on more of a gestalt [[Person/Pierre Schaeffer/Form Matter Pair]] than on the detail of the granulations.
		- The [[Mass]] of the grains is a part of the sound here as well; there is a viscosity, a density to the grains.
