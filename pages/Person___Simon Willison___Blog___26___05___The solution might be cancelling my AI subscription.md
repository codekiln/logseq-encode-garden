- # [The solution might be cancelling my AI subscription](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/31/the-solution-might-be-cancelling-my-ai-subscription/)
	- Link post by [[Person/Simon Willison]] on [[Person/David Wilson/Blog/26/05/The solution might be cancelling my AI subscription]].
	- ## Highlights
		- "I didn't mean to build most of these things... one hour later the result is not a quick script for X, nor is my problem solved." — [[Person/David Wilson]]
		- "This technology is **horrific** for attention. It's a thermonuclear ADHD amplifier." — [[Person/David Wilson]]
		- "A tool producing cheap reward with minimal input and no friction can only be a liability." — [[Person/David Wilson]]
	- ## [[My Notes]]
		- Willison finds the tension relatable — coding agents can take him from vague idea to working, documented project in under an hour, creating an overwhelming backlog of abandoned work.
		- Counterpoint surfaced in the [Hacker News discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48345896): people with ADHD reported opposite experiences, with AI agents actually improving focus and project completion rates.
			- > "... for me (also ADHD) it's kind of the opposite. I'm finishing side projects for the first time ever because I can actually get them working before I get bored of them"
			- > "As someone with ADHD I feel like AI is a salve for my mind. I used to listen to intense EDM while working. Now I sit in silence and talk to my agents. I maintain inbox zero. I absorb and comment across all relevant projects, even outside my team. I literally feel like I have a support team for the first time."
			- > "For those of us prone to hyperfocus, working with AI can provide the kinds of stimulation we crave. I can hardly remember a time when I've felt more engaged with my work, more productive, and more badass."
