- Not every distinction is worth recording.
	- Recording more is not always better. Each distinction you write down is something to maintain, read past, and keep consistent — a standing cost, whether or not it ever pays off.
	- Record a distinction only when you act on it differently. If two categories are handled the same way, the label separating them is friction with no return; collapse them.
	- ## [[Why]]
		- A distinction that changes no decision adds reading and upkeep while subtracting nothing.
		- This is [[My/Principle/Simplify/Minimize Surface Area]] applied to notes and schemas: every recorded category is surface area.
