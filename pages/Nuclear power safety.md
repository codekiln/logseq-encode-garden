- [[Nuclear power safety]] is the discipline of keeping nuclear technology safe across design, construction, operation, maintenance, training, emergency response, and disposal.
- [[Person/Hyman G. Rickover]]'s contribution was to make nuclear safety an engineering culture rather than a regulatory afterthought.
	- In the [[Nuclear Navy]], safety depended on technical competence, procedural rigor, personal accountability, documentation, and lifecycle ownership.
- Rickoverian safety doctrine emphasizes:
	- [[Design conservatism]]
	- [[Procedural discipline]]
	- [[Training and qualification]]
	- [[Personal accountability]]
	- [[Configuration control]]
	- [[Lifecycle ownership]]
- This is a useful comparison point for [[AI/Safety]].
	- In both cases, the hard safety problem is not only whether the technology can work.
	- It is whether the institution building and operating it can maintain competence, honesty, discipline, documentation, and authority under pressure.
