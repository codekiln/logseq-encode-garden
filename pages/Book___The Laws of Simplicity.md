- # The Ten Laws
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 01 - Reduce]] — The simplest way to achieve simplicity is through thoughtful reduction.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 02 - Organize]] — Organization makes a system of many appear fewer.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 03 - Time]] — Savings in time feel like simplicity.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 04 - Learn]] — Knowledge makes everything simpler.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 05 - Differences]] — Simplicity and complexity need each other.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 06 - Context]] — What lies in the periphery of simplicity is definitely not peripheral.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 07 - Emotion]] — More emotions are better than less.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 08 - Trust]] — In simplicity we trust.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 09 - Failure]] — Some things can never be made simple.
	- [[Book/The Laws of Simplicity/Law 10 - The One]] — Simplicity is about subtracting the obvious, and adding the meaningful.
