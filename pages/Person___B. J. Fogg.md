- [[Person/B. J. Fogg]]
	- **B.J. Fogg** (Brian Jeffrey Fogg, born August 7, 1963) is an American social scientist and researcher known for his work on persuasive technology and behavior design.
	- **Key Contributions:**
		- Coined the term **"captology"** (Computers As Persuasive Technologies) around 1996
		- Founded and directed the Stanford Persuasive Technology Lab (now Stanford Behavior Design Lab)
		- Developed the **Fogg Behavior Model (FBM)** which specifies that behavior happens when three elements converge: Motivation, Ability, and Trigger
		- Authored *Persuasive Technology: Using Computers to Change What We Think and Do* (2003)
		- His PhD thesis *"Charismatic Computers"* (1997) was among early works showing how interactive computing can influence users
	- **Education:**
		- BA and MA in English at Brigham Young University
		- Second MA and PhD in Communications at Stanford University
	- **Research Focus:**
		- Persuasive technology and captology
		- Behavior design and systematic methods for designing behavior change
		- Web credibility research
		- Mobile persuasion
