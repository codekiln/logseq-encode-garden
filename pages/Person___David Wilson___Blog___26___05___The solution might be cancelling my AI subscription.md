- # [The solution might be cancelling my AI subscription](https://thoughts.hmmz.org/2026-05-31.html)
	- ## [[My Notes]]
		- Wilson catalogued ~50 AI-assisted projects; almost none served a useful purpose.
		- Core thesis: friction equals focus, and focus produces actual products. A voice-to-blog pipeline experiment produced "unbridled garbage" because removing friction also removed deliberation.
		- Draws on [[Person/Cal Newport]]'s research: productivity tools often increase shallow tasks and context-switching rather than enabling deep, valuable work.
		- Proposes deliberate curtailment of AI use to restore focus and intentionality — not waiting for better tools or models.
	- ## Highlights
		- "I didn't mean to build most of these things... one hour later the result is not a quick script for X, nor is my problem solved."
		- "This technology is **horrific** for attention. It's a thermonuclear ADHD amplifier."
		- "Slopping out a 10,000 LOC untested Python/JS mess in 5 minutes helps nobody."
		- "Because the effort was removed, so was the commitment, and with the commitment the focus, and with the focus any meaningful product."
		- "A tool producing cheap reward with minimal input and no friction can only be a liability."
