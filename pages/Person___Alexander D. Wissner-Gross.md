- # Alexander D. Wissner-Gross
	- ## About
		- American research scientist and entrepreneur working across applied technology and theoretical physics; fellow at Harvard’s Institute for Applied Computational Science.
		- **Education:** S.B. degrees in physics, electrical engineering, and mathematics from MIT (2003); as of Wikipedia’s account, the last student to complete MIT’s triple major before the option ended. Ph.D. in physics from Harvard (2007), with graduate support including a Hertz Foundation fellowship and a Marshall Scholarship.
		- **Entrepreneurship:** In 2007 founded **CO2Stats** (Y Combinator–backed), which estimated carbon dioxide emissions from website use; drew public attention in a dispute over the environmental cost of a Google search (Wissner-Gross disputed a widely repeated emissions figure attributed to him).
		- **Research:** Work has included computational physics (e.g. with Efthimios Kaxiras on ice on sodium-coated diamond at body temperature, with possible implications for medical implants), discussion of physical latency limits in electronic trading, and—most widely cited—a 2013 *Physical Review Letters* paper with Cameron Freer on **“causal entropic forces,”** linking apparent goal-directed behavior to maximizing access to future states (entropy / “freedom of action”); popular coverage and substantial skeptical commentary followed.
