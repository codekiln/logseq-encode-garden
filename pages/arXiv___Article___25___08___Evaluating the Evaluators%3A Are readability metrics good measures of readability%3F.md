- # [Evaluating the Evaluators: Are readability metrics good measures of readability?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.19221)
	- arXiv:2508.19221 · Isabel Cachola, Daniel Khashabi, Mark Dredze (Johns Hopkins)
	- ## Summary
		- Surveys Plain Language Summarization (PLS) and finds the field's standard practice is to judge readability with traditional formulas like Flesch–Kincaid Grade Level (FKGL).
		- Tests 8 readability metrics against human judgments. Most, including FKGL, correlate poorly with what people actually find readable.
		- Language models judge readability better — the best model reaches a 0.56 Pearson correlation with human judgments — and capture deeper factors the formulas miss, such as the background knowledge a text demands.
		- Closes with recommendations for evaluating plain language summaries, and releases the analysis code and survey data.
	- ## Why it matters here
		- Direct evidence that readability formulas measure surface features, not comprehension — which is why [[My/Pref/Writing/Use Plain language]] treats the formula as a diagnostic, not a goal.
