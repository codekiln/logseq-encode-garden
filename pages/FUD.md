- # Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt
	- A persuasion tactic that frames alternatives as risky by emphasizing worst-case outcomes, uncertainty, or selective negatives.
	- ## History
		- The phrase predates computing and appeared in English usage before it became a technical term.
		- In 1970s enterprise computing, the acronym was popularized around competition in the IBM mainframe ecosystem.
		- The most cited origin story attributes the modern technical usage to [[Gene Amdahl]], describing how incumbent sales tactics could discourage customers from choosing competitors like [[Amdahl Corporation]].
		- Through the 1980s and 1990s, the term spread across personal computing and software platform battles, where it was used to describe competitive messaging intended to slow adoption of rival systems.
		- In the 2000s onward, FUD became general vocabulary beyond tech, commonly used for politics, security marketing, and public discourse whenever doubt-amplifying narratives are used strategically.
