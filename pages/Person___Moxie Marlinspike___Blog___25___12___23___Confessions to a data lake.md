- # [Confessions to a data lake](https://confer.to/blog/2025/12/confessions-to-a-data-lake/)
	- Author: [[Person/Moxie Marlinspike]]
	- Source: [[Confer]] Blog
	- ## Summary
		- The post argues that AI chat interfaces invite people to reveal unfinished thoughts, uncertainties, and private reasoning patterns in a way search boxes and social feeds do not.
		- Moxie contrasts the intimate feel of an assistant conversation with the server-side reality of plaintext data stores, business partners, future advertisers, hackers, lawyers, and governments potentially gaining access.
		- Confer is presented as a response: an end-to-end encrypted AI chat service meant to let people explore ideas without turning their private thinking into training data, subpoenable records, or ad-targeting context.
	- ## Notes
		- The central security claim is interface honesty: if an interface feels like a private chat, the underlying system should provide privacy properties that match that feeling.
		- The post treats AI assistant privacy as especially urgent because the medium encourages users to expose how they think, not only what they have already decided.
		- It connects AI chat privacy to the earlier mainstreaming of end-to-end encrypted messaging through Signal.
