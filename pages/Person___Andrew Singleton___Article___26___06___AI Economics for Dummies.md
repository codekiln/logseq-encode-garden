- # [AI Economics for Dummies](https://www.mcsweeneys.net/articles/ai-economics-for-dummies)
	- Author: **[[Person/Andrew Singleton]]**
	- Source: [McSweeney's Internet Tendency](https://www.mcsweeneys.net/)
	- Journal link: [[2026/06/17]]
	- ## Summary
		- Satirical "for Dummies" explainer on AI-company economics, framed around the AI giants getting ready to go public. Five word-problem vignettes lampoon circular revenue, subscriptions sold below cost, round-tripped investment booked as revenue, automation that destroys more value than it creates, and debt-fueled overbuilding—each capped by financial journalists and Wall Street abandoning scrutiny for worship.
	- ## Notes
		- Running joke: the more nonsensical the economics, the more reverent the financial press (WSJ, Forbes, Goldman Sachs) becomes—escalating to literal religion and holy war.
		- Vignette logic mirrors real bubble critiques: the grape arbitrage = revenue recognized up front against circular sales; the taxi subscription = selling below marginal cost (gas spend exceeds revenue); the crematorium-and-propane deal = round-tripping an investment back as reported revenue; the mule farm = automation overpromised while productivity is destroyed; the unwanted apartments = capex overbuild on faith.
		- Closes by telling the still-confused reader that everything is working, everyone is making money, and to "stop asking questions, luddite."
