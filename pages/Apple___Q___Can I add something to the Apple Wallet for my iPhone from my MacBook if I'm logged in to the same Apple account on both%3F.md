- # Can I add something to the Apple Wallet for my iPhone from my MacBook if I'm logged in to the same Apple account on both?
	- ## My Notes
		- Distinguish **passes/tickets** (boarding passes, event tickets, coupons) from **payment cards** (debit/credit for Apple Pay); Apple documents them differently.
	- ## Answer
		- [[AI/Response]]
			- **It depends what “something” is.** Being signed in with the same Apple Account on both devices is necessary for several Wallet-related features, but it does **not** mean every Wallet item you add on the Mac automatically appears on the iPhone.
			- **Passes and tickets (non–payment “Wallet passes”)** — **Often yes.** Apple documents adding passes (for example boarding passes or movie tickets) from **Safari on Mac** to Wallet on **iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, or Mac**, and says **[iCloud must be set up for Safari](https://support.apple.com/guide/icloud/mm5400ef10c4)** on the iPhone (iOS 6 or later) **or Mac**. You open the pass in Safari on the Mac, use **Add to Wallet** (or **Update** if the pass changed). Mail on Mac can similarly surface passes you add toward Wallet on iPhone when iCloud is set up on both devices (see Apple’s Mail User Guide for your macOS version).
			- **Debit/credit and other Apple Pay payment cards** — **Not by simple cross-device sync.** To use Apple Pay on more than one device, Apple’s **Set up Apple Pay** guidance is to **add the card in Wallet (or Wallet & Apple Pay settings) on each device** you want to pay from, then complete any **bank or card issuer verification** for that device. The same Apple Account helps (and **Cards Found For You** / similar flows can reuse cards you already added elsewhere so you are not retyping everything), but the card is still **enrolled per device** for security, not copied automatically as a fully active card everywhere.
			- **Practical takeaway** — For **tickets/passes from the web or email**, adding on the Mac in Safari or Mail is a normal path and can land in Wallet on the iPhone when iCloud requirements are met. For **paying in stores or with Apple Pay cards**, plan to **add (or re-verify) the card on the iPhone** even if it is already on the Mac.
