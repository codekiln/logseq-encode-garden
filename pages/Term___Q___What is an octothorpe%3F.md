- # What is an octothorpe?
	- ## [[AI Answer]]
		- [[AI/Response]]
			- **Short answer:** An octothorpe is the `#` symbol — the character also called the hash, pound sign, or number sign.
			- **Etymology:** Coined at [[Bell Labs]] in the late 1960s when `#` was added to the touch-tone telephone keypad. *Octo-* refers to the symbol's eight points; the *-thorpe* part is disputed — one Bell Labs account says engineer Don MacPherson added it in honor of Olympic athlete Jim Thorpe, while another traces it to the Old English *thorp* (village).
			- **Other names for `#`:** hash, pound sign (US), number sign, crosshatch, and informally "hashtag" (which strictly names the `#`-prefixed phrase, not the symbol itself). The musical sharp ♯ is a different glyph.
			- **In code-review remarks** like "the scenario should require the rationale, not the octothorpe," it refers to the literal `#` character — i.e., a check should assert on the comment's explanatory text rather than the mere presence of a `#` comment marker (or Markdown heading marker).
			- Refs: [Merriam-Webster — octothorpe](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/octothorpe), [Wikipedia — Number sign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Number_sign)
