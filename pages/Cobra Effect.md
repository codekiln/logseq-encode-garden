- # Cobra Effect
	- **Statement**: an incentive meant to solve a problem makes it worse, because people optimize for the reward in unintended ways.
	- **Origin**: colonial India—a bounty on dead cobras led people to breed cobras for the reward; when the bounty ended, the breeders released their now-worthless snakes, worsening the infestation.
	- **Kind**: named effect (perverse incentive).
	- **Example**: paying for dead cobras leads people to breed cobras.
	- ## See also
		- [[Goodhart's Law]], [[Campbell's Law]] — related failures of optimizing for a proxy.
