- # Campbell's Law
	- **Statement**: the more a quantitative social indicator is used for decision-making, the more it is subject to corruption pressures and the more it distorts the social processes it was meant to monitor.
	- **Origin**: social scientist Donald T. Campbell (1979).
	- **Kind**: heuristic.
	- **Example**: schools teaching to standardized tests until the scores no longer reflect genuine learning.
