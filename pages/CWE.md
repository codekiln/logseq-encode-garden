- # [CWE](https://cwe.mitre.org/)
	- ## Overview
		- [[CWE]] (Common Weakness Enumeration) is a community-developed, freely available taxonomy of common **weakness types** in software and hardware — the categories of flaw that lead to exploitable [[Security/Vulnerability]] instances.
		- It provides a common language for describing and classifying weaknesses so that tools, advisories, and researchers can talk about the same defect consistently.
	- ## Normative links
		- Official site: [cwe.mitre.org](https://cwe.mitre.org/)
		- Each weakness has a stable **CWE-ID** and definition page, e.g. [CWE-367 (TOCTOU)](https://cwe.mitre.org/data/definitions/367.html), [CWE-79 (XSS)](https://cwe.mitre.org/data/definitions/79.html), [CWE-89 (SQL injection)](https://cwe.mitre.org/data/definitions/89.html).
		- Published as versioned releases (the 4.x series), with the full list browsable by research, development, and architectural views.
	- ## Scope
		- Classifies **types** of weaknesses (the underlying flaw class), distinct from [CVE](https://www.cve.org/), which identifies **specific instances** of vulnerabilities in particular products.
	- ## Maintainer
		- Maintained by [[MITRE]] and sponsored by the U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA).
	- ## Related
		- [CWE Top 25 Most Dangerous Software Weaknesses](https://cwe.mitre.org/top25/) — an annually published ranking of the most impactful weakness types.
