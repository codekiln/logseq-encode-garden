- # [Dopamine Fracking](https://igerman.cc/blog/dopamine-fracking/)
	- ## [[My Notes]]
		- Coins the term "dopamine fracking": pumping immense, disproportionate resources into a previously casual or complex activity to forcefully extract the purest dopamine hit, with no regard for anything else.
		- Draws on [[Person/Metta Beshay]]'s research on drugs in cultural context — when activities are industrialized purely for dopamine extraction, their complexity and meaning vanish.
		- The strawberry analogy: isolating a single aromatic compound removes texture, juiciness, complexity — "about 500 individual human experiences" — replacing diverse, imperfect experiences with identical synthetic ones.
		- Capitalism ensures "Everything converges on a single point, there's quite literally no room for anything else."
		- Connects to [[Person/David Wilson/Blog/26/05/The solution might be cancelling my AI subscription]] on AI as a friction-removal tool that erases commitment and focus.
		- Proposed remedy: awareness and personal limits — deliberately avoiding content that exploits dopaminergic triggers.
