- # **PEBCAK** = *“Problem Exists Between Chair And Keyboard.”*
	- It’s informal IT/helpdesk slang used to indicate that the issue isn’t with the system, but with the user.
	- ## Origin
		- Emerged in **1990s IT and tech support culture**, especially among sysadmins and helpdesk workers.
		- Part of a broader family of humorous “user error” acronyms (e.g., ID10T error, PICNIC).
		- No single documented inventor; it spread organically through:
			- early **Usenet groups**
			- internal corporate IT jargon
			- later forums like **Slashdot** and similar communities
		- ### Variants
			- **PEBKAC** (more common spelling)
			- **PEBCAK**
			- Same meaning, different letter order conventions
		- ### Related terms
			- **PICNIC**: *Problem In Chair, Not In Computer*
			- **ID10T error**: “idiot error” disguised as a code
