- # Moore's Law
	- **Statement**: the number of transistors on an integrated circuit roughly doubles about every two years, with cost per transistor falling accordingly.
	- **Origin**: Gordon Moore (1965, revised 1975), co-founder of Intel; an observation and industry projection, not a law of nature.
	- **Kind**: empirical trend.
	- **Why it belongs here**: often cited as if it were an axiom, but it is an observed regularity that has been slowing as physical limits approach—an illustration of how a named "law" can expire.
