- # Eliane Radigue
	- ## Bio
		- French composer associated with electroacoustic music, electronic music, drone, and minimalist composition.
		- Born Éliane Louise Thérèse Radigue on [[1932-01-24 Sun]] in Paris; died on [[2026-02-23 Mon]] in Paris at age 94.
		- Studied and worked in the orbit of [[Person/Pierre Schaeffer]] and [[Person/Pierre Henry]], then developed her own approach through tape, feedback, and long-form electronic works.
		- Best known for decades of work with an ARP 2500 synthesizer she named Jules, and for later acoustic collaborations shaped by intense listening and sustained tone.
		- Her Tibetan Buddhist practice deeply informed works such as *Trilogie de la Mort*, *Songs of Milarepa*, *Jetsun Mila*, *Naldjorlak*, and the expansive *Occam* series.
