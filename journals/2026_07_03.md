# so much [[Word/Salad]]
	- ugh. AI keeps writing things into my plans to "never" do something that's completely unrelated, e.g. while building an iPhone app, "never talk to a brown cow" (it's usually not that unrelated - but that's how it feels). So often, AI expressions collapse into an always/never binary.
	- so many double negatives.
	- > That's why example 1 (wrap/linebreak) felt like a non-example to you — an unfamiliar reader doesn't obviously know what linebreak does, so spelling it out is earning its place under the real preference, even though the spec's current wording would tell an agent to omit it.
	- regarding [[My/AI/Rule/No Recipe in the Cake]] - new [[My/AI/Rule/Prune useless commandments]]
		- ```
		  ❯ in what universe does this make sense?
		  
		    > ### Requirement: Link rationale is not narrated into the config
		  
		    > A config comment carries the garden link. It SHALL NOT explain the linking rule itself
		    — for example, why the link points at an entity page rather than a preference page.
		    Comments can still say what a file or setting is; they just don't explain the linking
		    convention.
		  
		  
		    to me, this is just terrible. It's so terrible I can't even really articulate what
		    preference I should add to my knowledge garden so as to guide AI away from this. I keep
		    noticing that AI puts into rules explicit instructions not to do things ... that it
		    should be obvious one should never do. Imagine a parenting guidebook that said, "when
		    teaching your child how to tie their shoes, you must never push them into the water."
		    the instruction in the 2nd part of the phrase doesn't have anything to do with the
		    first part of the phrase. In what world would it make sense to make a comment that
		    itself explains how and why the comment is being made?
		  
		  ⏺ You've named the actual disease, not just a symptom. This requirement only exists
		    because AI narrates its own instruction-following into things it produces. But "don't
		    narrate why you added a link" isn't a fact about how garden links work — it's a symptom
		    of that one general AI failure mode, bolted onto this one spec as if it were a
		    link-formatting rule. Your shoe-tying analogy is exactly right: the two halves of the
		    sentence have no real relationship to each other except that AI needed reminding not to
		    misbehave, and someone decided the reminder belonged wherever the misbehavior happened
		    to show up last.
		  
		    The general fix already exists, once, in the one place it belongs: the "No Recipe in the
		    Cake" AI rule, loaded into every session via .claude/rules/. A domain spec about how
		    links work shouldn't also carry a copy of "and don't narrate yourself while doing this"
		    — that's not a property of links, it's a standing constraint on AI's writing that
		    applies everywhere, all the time, already.
		  
		    I think the right move is to delete this requirement entirely from garden-entity-links
		   
		  ```
