# [[Ideas]]
	- ## Banning AI from using specific words or phrases
		- I'd like to make a system that makes it very easy for me to ban AI from using words and phrases with me or when creating documents.
			- My first thought about this was to make an [[AI/Agent/Skill]] in [[rulesync]] which would let me quickly add a word or phrase to a part of the knowledge garden that catalogs the words or phrases that I'd like AI to avoid when communicating with me or on my behalf, following progressive disclosure. I was thinking that ideally, there would be a table of contents page that would have logseq wikilinks to individual pages, one per word or phrase. That way listing the contents of a namespace would be sufficient to tell the AI what to ignore.
			- Later, I was thinking of using [[Claude/Code/Hook]]s for this.
	- ## Convert `#tag` links to `[[wikilink]]` form on commit
		- I'd like a pre-commit converter, built like the `text:fix-invisible` mise task, that rewrites Logseq `#tag` and `#[[Page]]` links into `[[...]]` form — one obvious way to link ([[Py/Zen of Python]]), and the shape [[Looksyk]] can import, since it reads `[[...]]` but not `#tag`.
		- Not as safe a rewrite as invisible whitespace, so it stays deferred. A `#` also begins headings, hex colors, shell comments, and URL fragments, so the converter has to skip fenced and inline code, ignore `#` before a space or another `#`, and treat `#` as a link only at a word boundary. Scope it to `.md` files under pages/ and journals/, where well over a thousand inline `#` links live today.
